You've done the ritual before: "Storage Almost Full" pops up, so you open Photos and start deleting things you love just to make room for things you need.

The problem is you're doing surgery with a butter knife. You're guessing which photos matter based on memory, mood, and how quickly you want the notification to disappear. Meanwhile the actual bloat — the four identical photos from one burst, the video you AirDropped to yourself and forgot, the meme saved twice from two different chats — sits there untouched, because nothing on your phone tells you it's there.

Duplicates aren't a guess. They're a fingerprint.

A photo doesn't need a human to know it's identical to another photo. SpaceMint reads the actual file data — its digital fingerprint — and matches it byte for byte. Two files either share the same fingerprint or they don't. There's no "looks similar," no risk of a near-duplicate that's actually a completely different memory getting swept away by accident.

If it's flagged as a duplicate, it's mathematically identical. Not close. Identical.
